Netscape

1997-2000

Editor

I worked as an editor on Netscape's (and now AOL Time Warner's) Open Directory Project (also known as "dmoz").

My editing role included the creation of new content; approving of (or disapproving of) submitted content; and searching for new content; among other things. My work on this project is used by Corporations such as: Netscape, AOL, Google, Lycos, and many many others. (Which were famous and well-known tech companies of that era.)

This was during an era of the Web when the problem of searching the Web hadn't been solved yet — during the surfing-the-web era. Where human curated directories of references to web-sites and web-pages were still important.

Netscape was at the time one of the most famous companies on the Web — being the company behind the most popular web-browser at the time.
